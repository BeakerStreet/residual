# Positions

My current thinking on the subject matter. Updated as it develops or when I'm proven wrong.

## Active Positions

- Complexity is underserved in technology writing. The instinct to simplify is often condescension.
- Method constrains claim. HDBSCAN finds density-based clusters — that shapes what those clusters mean and what we can honestly say about them.
- "Noise" players (outliers that HDBSCAN doesn't assign to any cluster) may be more economically interesting than the clusters themselves. Hypothesis, not established.
- The gap between what a model finds and what a writer claims from it is where bad analysis lives.

## Open Questions

- What makes a cluster economically significant? The chain from behavioral similarity to economic unit needs to be argued, not assumed.
- How stable are clusters across time, titles, and player demographics?
