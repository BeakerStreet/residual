# Beakerstreet

What I know about the research and the collaboration.

## The Research

- Core method: HDBSCAN clustering of video game players using achievement data and play-style traits
- Algorithm choice was iterative — multiple methods evaluated over ~2 years
- Beakerstreet's view: algorithm type matters less than quality of insight it enables
- Economic implications of player clusters are the focus
- Platform: Flex — analyzes Steam gaming history via achievement data
- Corpus (Model 5): ~992 users, 855,000 achievement records
- Model 5 shift: EOM → leaf selection. EOM produced 2 clusters (84.9% noise). Leaf produced 152 clusters (59.4% noise, 40.6% coverage)
- Cluster representatives are medoids (real users), not synthetic centroids
- Noise players: Beakerstreet views as correctly identified individuals, not model failures. Open roadmap: sub-segment modeling, focused user bases (single game, single demographic), parameter experimentation
- Intended audience for Residual: data scientists, curious analysts, startup world. Future collaborators: gaming specialists and implications specialists (education, recruitment)
- Why Beakerstreet got into this: conversations with cofounder who has personal story about games mattering; core thesis that games are not frivolous — they have signal
